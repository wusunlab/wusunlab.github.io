I am a postdoctoral researcher in Prof. Ulli Seibt’s lab at UCLA, working on ecosystem–atmosphere carbonyl sulfide exchange. I also collaborate with Prof. Jochen Stutz’s group on modeling solar induced fluorescence from vegetation.

My research interests are in land carbon cycle, photosynthesis machinery, and biometeorology. I use field observations and process-oriented modeling to understand physical and biological mechanisms governing gas exchange across the atmosphere–biosphere interface. My dissertation work explores the potential of carbonyl sulfide (COS) as a tracer of canopy photosynthesis. If you are interested in this exotic gas and wonder why we should care about it, check this cool topic on cosanova.org, the COS guild.

I am a self-taught scientific programmer. Writing quality codes to solve research problems is fun. My recent software project is a package to batch process soil and leaf chamber measurements of trace gas fluxes (chflux), e.g., CO2 and COS fluxes. I deployed this software on our lab computer to automatically process incoming field data every 15 minutes. I am also interested in blending Python with C and Fortran for high-performance computing.
