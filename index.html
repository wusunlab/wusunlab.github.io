I am a postdoctoral researcher in Ulli Seibt’s lab. My research focuses on the carbon cycle in terrestrial ecosystems. With the accelerating trends of climate change, it is uncertain whether the terrestrial biosphere will continue serving as a carbon sink. To answer this big question requires an understanding of how photosynthesis responds to climate. My research has been dedicated to developing novel tracer techniques for photosynthesis observations — carbonyl sulfide and solar induced chlorophyll fluorescence. These two tracers are each associated with a different stage of photosynthesis and are therefore promising in revealing fundamental mechanisms that control ecosystem photosynthesis.

I received my Ph.D. in Atmospheric Science from UCLA in 2017. My dissertation work focused on the soil–atmosphere exchange of carbonyl sulfide (COS) using chamber observations and reactive transport modeling. I received my B.Sc. in Geology from Nanjing University in 2012.

Besides my main research activities, I am interested in scientific programming and data science. I use Python, Julia, and other high-level programming languages to solve my research problems, and occasionally contribute to my own software packages.

Research interests

  • Carbonyl sulfide as a tracer for leaf gas exchange
  • Solar induced chlorophyll fluorescence as a tracer for photosynthetic light reactions
  • Reactive transport modeling of soil–atmosphere exchange of trace gases
  • Climate change impacts on the terrestrial carbon cycle
